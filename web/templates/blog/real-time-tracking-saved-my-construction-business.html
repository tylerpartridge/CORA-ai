How Real-Time Tracking Saved My Construction Business

My name is Mike Thompson. I'm a general contractor in Denver. Eighteen months ago, I was three weeks away from closing my business. Today, I'm more profitable than ever. This is the story of how real-time tracking saved everything I'd built.

The Breaking Point

January 2024. I was sitting in my truck outside Home Depot, staring at my bank balance: $1,247. Payroll was due in 5 days: $8,400.

I'd just finished three big jobs. Should have made $45,000 profit. Instead, I was broke.

The Terrifying Reality:

  • Busy all year, but no money in the bank
  • Couldn't figure out where profits went
  • Spreadsheets showed I should be profitable
  • Reality: losing $3-5k per job

I wasn't alone. 62% of construction businesses fail within 5 years. Most never know what hit them.

The Spreadsheet Lie

Every Sunday night, I'd update my spreadsheets. Everything looked fine on paper:

Johnson Bathroom Remodel (What My Spreadsheet Said):

  • Contract: $28,000
  • Materials: $11,000
  • Labor: $8,000
  • Expected Profit: $9,000 (32%)

But spreadsheets only show what you remember to enter. And I was forgetting a lot.

The Real-Time Reality Check

In desperation, I started tracking every expense in real-time with CORA. Within 48 hours, I discovered the truth:

Johnson Bathroom Remodel (The Real Numbers):

  • Contract: $28,000
  • Materials (initial): $11,000
  • Materials (additional runs): $2,847 ← Never tracked these
  • Labor (quoted): $8,000
  • Labor (callbacks/fixes): $1,920 ← Invisible hours
  • Truck/fuel: $340 ← 17 supply runs
  • Tool rental: $185 ← Forgot to bill client
  • Disposal fees: $220 ← Always forgot these
  • Actual Profit: $3,488 (12.4%)
I lost $5,512 in profit on ONE JOB

Multiply that by 20 jobs per year. That's $110,000 in lost profit. No wonder I was broke.

The First 30 Days of Truth

Real-time tracking revealed patterns I'd been blind to:

Week 1 Discoveries:

  • Making 3-4 supply runs per job (not 1)
  • Callbacks eating 10% of labor hours
  • Disposal fees on EVERY job
  • Travel time never billed

Week 1 Changes:

  • Started charging for supply runs
  • Added disposal fees to contracts
  • Tracked callback hours
  • Billed portal-to-portal time

By week 2, I was seeing profit warnings in real-time:

⚠️ CORA Alert: "Miller kitchen at 67% material budget with 40% work remaining"

That alert saved me $3,200. I immediately talked to the client about a change order instead of eating the cost.

The 90-Day Transformation

Three months of real-time tracking changed everything:

Before (January 2024):

  • Profit margin: 8%
  • Cash in bank: $1,247
  • Monthly profit: -$2,000 (losing money)
  • Stress level: Couldn't sleep
  • Time on paperwork: 10 hrs/week

After (April 2024):

  • Profit margin: 22%
  • Cash in bank: $31,420
  • Monthly profit: $9,800
  • Stress level: Sleep like a baby
  • Time on paperwork: 15 min/week
175% Profit Increase in 90 Days

The Specific Changes That Saved My Business

  1. Voice tracking everything: "Hey CORA, bought screws $12 for Miller job" - tracked 147 forgotten expenses in first month
  2. Real-time margin monitoring: Know instantly when a job goes sideways, not 3 weeks later
  3. Automatic categorization: Every expense tagged to the right job, no more guessing
  4. Daily profit snapshots: See exactly how much I made (or lost) each day
  5. Change order alerts: When costs exceed budget, I know immediately

What I Learned (The Hard Way)

Truth #1: You're Not Tracking 40% of Your Expenses

Small stuff adds up. That $8 parking, $15 hardware store run, $30 in gas - it's killing your profits.

Truth #2: Spreadsheets Are Always Wrong

They only show what you remember to enter. Real-time tracking shows what actually happened.

Truth #3: You Need to Know TODAY, Not Sunday

By the time you update that spreadsheet, you've already lost money. Real-time means real profit.

One Year Later

Today, August 2025, my business is unrecognizable:

  • Annual revenue: $1.8M (up from $750K)
  • Net profit: 24% (up from 8%)
  • Employees: 7 (up from 3)
  • Cash reserves: $127,000 (up from $1,247)
  • Stress level: Actually take weekends off now

The difference? I know my numbers in real-time. No surprises. No mystery losses. No more sleepless nights.

My Advice to Every Contractor

If you're busy but broke, you're not bad at construction. You're bad at tracking.

Start tracking everything in real-time. Not tomorrow. Not Sunday. NOW. Your business depends on it.

Mine did.
